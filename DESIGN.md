## Problem Statement

Indian preschools (Pre-K, Nursery, LKG, UKG) are underserved by existing software. The current status quo is one of two patterns:

1. **Standalone preschool chains** (the primary target): Running on WhatsApp groups + spreadsheets + paper registers. No structured ERP at all. Possibly using Seesaw for parent engagement, but Seesaw is expensive and US-market-centric.

2. **Pre-K wings of larger schools**: Using a generic K-12 ERP that was never designed for early years — missing developmental milestones, play-based learning logs, daily activity feeds, and high-frequency parent communication.

3. **Using Seasaw or Illumine** : Using Seasaw or Illumine which are very expensive softwares and have a learning curve as well as onboarding friction. These days schools also want an instant onboarding too via direct signup for trials as well as payment enabled on subscriptions. 

There is no purpose-built, India-first, design-quality preschool management OS in the market. Illumine (Bengaluru, $2.5M seed, 3,000+ centers) is the closest funded competitor, but has not yet demonstrated Seesaw-level UX quality or deep India-specific features.

## Demand Evidence

- **One specific pilot school** (existing Cadence Infotech contact) is ready to pilot this product — the strongest pre-product demand signal possible.
- Multiple existing Cadence Infotech school ERP customers have explicitly asked for a Pre-K module or flagged that their Pre-K wing is unserved.
- The documented market pain: generic ERP + Seesaw is the current "best practice" among higher-income preschools, but the dual-tool cost and complexity is a clear consolidation opportunity.
- School owners' primary buying trigger: **competition** (nearby schools with Seesaw look more modern during admissions) + **cost** (paying for two tools).

## Status Quo

**Standalone preschool chain owners (primary target):**
- Parent communication: WhatsApp groups (free, familiar, impossible to audit or brand)
- Attendance: Paper registers or basic spreadsheets
- Fees: Tally / manual invoicing / cash receipts
- Learning progress: Paper portfolios or nothing
- Admissions: Walk-ins, phone calls, no CRM

**Higher-end preschools (secondary):**
- Generic ERP (Entab, EduSys, or similar) for fees/admin
- Seesaw for classroom activity feed + parent engagement
- Total cost: ₹10K–₹25K ERP + Seesaw subscription (~$8–$12/teacher/month = ₹8K–₹15K/year/teacher)
- Illumine pricing is Rs 800 to Rs 1500 per student per annum. 
- However this wont be a major market for now.

**What this costs them:** Time (30+ min/day per teacher on WhatsApp), money (two SaaS subscriptions), perception (looks less premium than competitors), and data (no structured learning records to show parents at year-end).

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**Primary buyer:** Standalone preschool chain owner running 2-10 centers in India. Does not have an existing school ERP. Competing locally for enrollments. Wants to look premium without paying Seesaw pricing.

**Secondary buyer:** Large school chain owner who has a K-12 ERP but no Pre-K coverage.

**Narrowest wedge (first thing to ship and sell):**
> **The parent-teacher daily activity feed** — a mobile-first app where teachers post photos/videos of classroom activities and parents see them in a beautiful, branded feed. Free or near-free tier. This is the "WhatsApp killer" feature and the daily habit that drives adoption. However we should be able to provide limited feeds per day only. 

Once parents and teachers are using the feed daily, the upsell path is natural:
1. Milestone tracker 
2. Attendance + fee management (ERP layer)
3. Full preschool OS with admissions, reports, lesson plans

## Constraints

- Must be **mobile-first** — both teachers and parents in India use smartphones as primary devices, We definitely need a native mobile app for both iOS and Android.
- Must work on **low-bandwidth** — progressive loading, compressed media; full offline-first (local-first data sync) is a Phase 2 requirement, not launch
- Launch with English as a main language only for the app.
- Subsequently pilot : **Regional language support:** Hindi + Tamil at launch (pilot market); Marathi, Telugu, Kannada in Phase 2 (Month 6–12). Do not claim 5-language support as a moat until it is live. Also we need to go to Dubai so Arabic would be the next language to pilot. 
- Pricing must be accessible: **₹15,000–₹40,000/school/year** — this is a working assumption based on market research (₹10K current average ERP + Seesaw delta). Actual pricing to be validated with first 3 paying customers before setting list price.
- Must handle the **WhatsApp habit-switching problem** — the product needs to be more convenient than WhatsApp, not just better
- **DPDP Act 2023 compliance** — product handles photos and videos of minors; requires explicit parental consent flows, data residency in India, and documented data retention/deletion policy
- Distribution: leverage Cadence Infotech's existing school relationships for first 20 customers.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: "Seesaw for India" — Parent Engagement First
Build the parent-teacher daily activity feed as a standalone mobile app. Free tier. Add ERP modules only after adoption. Win the WhatsApp habit first, upsell the ERP later. There should be a relay to Whatsapp as notifications also that should be possible. 

- **Effort:** M | **Risk:** Medium
- **Pros:** Viral distribution, low adoption barrier, daily active usage before any contract
- **Cons:** Lower initial revenue, standalone apps are easy to copy, WhatsApp switching is hard
- **Why not chosen:** Delays ERP revenue; the user's team already has ERP infrastructure — use it from day one

### Approach B: All-in-One Preschool OS with Approach A Entry Point (CHOSEN)
Build the complete preschool management platform: admissions, fees, attendance, parent feed (Seesaw-style), milestone tracking, lesson plans, staff management — purpose-built for Indian preschools, interactive quizzes, library for kids, day-care features such as live camera tracking, school transportation management (school bus + tracking), Infirmary, Warehouse + Supplies, HRMS for school (for HR management of school including attendance of employees, payroll, leave management, performance etc), Teacher Training Module, Website Management Module, Admission Management Module with CRM Integrations etc. .

- **Effort:** XL | **Risk:** High
- **Pros:** Clean "one tool" pitch, highest ACV (₹15K–₹40K/year/school), Cadence's existing ERP infra accelerates build
- **Cons:** Illumine is the direct competitor with $2.5M and 3,000+ centers; long initial build

### Approach C: Franchise Chain Vertical
Skip individual schools. Target Eurokids, Kidzee, Tree House, Bachpan, Podar Jumbo Kids (combined 10,000+ centers). One enterprise deal = massive scale.

- **Effort:** L | **Risk:** Low-Medium
- **Pros:** One Eurokids deal = ~1,200 schools overnight
- **Cons:** 6–18 month sales cycle; franchise chains demand customization and have leverage

## Design System

### Visual Identity — Warm & Editorial

The parent feed and teacher app use a warm, editorial aesthetic. This is a deliberate choice against the generic edtech card grid. Every pixel should feel like a premium family photo book.

**Palette:**
- Background: `#FAF7F2` (warm cream — not pure white)
- Surface cards: `#FFFFFF` with `box-shadow: 0 1px 4px rgba(0,0,0,0.06)`
- Primary accent: `#5C8B6E` (muted sage green — school/nature, not clinical blue)
- Secondary accent: `#C4784A` (terracotta — warmth, India-rooted)
- Text primary: `#1A1A1A`
- Text secondary: `#6B6B6B`
- Destructive: `#D94F3D`

**Typography:**
- Display / captions: `Fraunces` (serif, warm) or `DM Serif Display` — used for AI-drafted post captions, milestone names, child names in portfolio
- Body / UI: `Inter` (sans-serif) — navigation, labels, form inputs
- Minimum body size: 16px on mobile (Indian users often have accessibility text sizes enabled)

**Spacing scale:** 4px base unit. Tokens: 4, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64.

**Component library:** React Native Paper (v5) for native components + custom design tokens for brand layer. Do NOT use default Material You palette — override all colors with the palette above. Web (Next.js admin): shadcn/ui with the same token overrides.

**Photo treatment:**
- Parent feed: Full-bleed, edge-to-edge photos. No rounded corners on photos (editorial, not card-in-card).
- Aspect ratio: 4:3 (standard camera roll). Crop center-fill on portrait photos.
- Video: 16:9 with play overlay. Auto-play muted on feed scroll, unmute on tap.

## Interaction State Specifications

Every screen must implement all four states. These are not afterthoughts — they are features.

### Parent Feed

| State | Design |
|-------|--------|
| **Loading** | Skeleton cards — warm cream rectangles, pulsing. NOT a spinner. |
| **Empty (first time)** | Illustration of a classroom + "Your child's first update will appear here. Teachers post daily." No CTA (school hasn't posted yet — not parent's fault). |
| **Empty (child not enrolled)** | "Ask your school to add you to [School Name]." + WhatsApp link to school's number. |
| **Error (network)** | "Couldn't load — check your connection." + Retry button. Last loaded content stays visible (stale-while-revalidate). |
| **No posts today** | "All quiet so far today. Check back after morning circle!" |

### Teacher Post Composer

| State | Design |
|-------|--------|
| **Loading AI caption** | Inline shimmer in the caption box: "Drafting caption..." — NOT a full-screen loader. Teacher can type over it immediately. |
| **AI caption ready** | Caption appears in italic with a subtle ✨ icon. "Edit or post as-is." |
| **Face recognition pending** | Per-child tag chips show as grey/dashed: "Identifying..." Never block posting. |
| **Face recognition failed** | "Couldn't auto-tag — add children manually." Grey chips become tappable. |
| **Upload in progress** | Progress bar in the FAB area. Feed optimistically shows post immediately (blurred if upload < 80%). |
| **Upload failed** | Toast: "Upload failed. Tap to retry." Post remains in draft queue. |

### Admin Dashboard Engagement Score

| State | Design |
|-------|--------|
| **First week (no history)** | Score shown, but trend arrow hidden. "Come back next week to see your trend." |
| **Score < 30%** | Score displayed in terracotta (#C4784A). Alert badge. "3 classes haven't posted this week." |
| **Score ≥ 70%** | Score displayed in sage green (#5C8B6E). Positive reinforcement: "Parents are loving it!" |
| **No enrolled parents** | "Add parents to start measuring engagement." Direct link to enrollment. |

## Critical User Journeys

### Journey 1: Teacher Posts a Daily Activity (The Core Loop)

```
Teacher opens app (home screen = class feed)
  → FAB (+ icon, bottom right, always visible)
  → Camera roll opens (system sheet, full-screen)
  → Teacher selects 1-4 photos or 1 video
  → AI begins processing in background (non-blocking)
  → Preview screen:
      - Full-bleed photo preview
      - AI-drafted caption in Fraunces font (editable)
      - Child tags as chips (suggested by Rekognition, teacher taps to confirm/remove)
      - Class selector (defaults to teacher's primary class)
      - Milestone tags (optional — teacher can skip)
  → [POST →] button
  → Optimistic post appears in feed
  → WhatsApp notification sent to parents of tagged children
```

Tap count: 3 (FAB → select photo → Post). AI caption review is the 3rd tap, not an additional one.
Edge case: teacher wants to post WITHOUT any child tagged → allowed. Post goes to all class parents.
Edge case: teacher posts video > 50MB → auto-compress client-side before upload (target: < 10MB).

### Journey 2: Parent Opens Magic-Link for First Time

```
Parent receives WhatsApp message:
  "Rohan had a great morning! [School name] shared an update.
   See it here: [magic-link]"
  → Parent taps link
  → Web view opens (no install required)
  → IMMEDIATELY shows: school header + post photo + caption + "Rohan" tag
  → Parent taps ❤️ Love (single tap, no login required for first reaction)
  → After reaction: soft prompt "Want to see all of Rohan's updates?"
  → After 3 views: gentle install nudge banner (dismissible, never modal)
  → On install: DPDP consent screen → set notification preferences → feed
```

Critical: The first reaction (❤️) requires NO LOGIN. Capture the emotional moment before asking for commitment.

### Journey 3: DPDP Consent at Parent Onboarding

```
Parent installs app (or proceeds past 3rd web view)
  → Consent screen (school-branded header):
      "Your child's privacy is our first commitment."
      Per-type checkboxes (all pre-checked, individually revocable):
        ☑ Share daily updates with you only
        ☑ Recognise [child name] in class photos (AI — server-side)
        ☑ Store data in India (deleted when you leave)
      [See full privacy policy] → opens modal, not new screen
      [I agree →]
  → Notification preferences (daily digest vs. per-post)
  → Feed
```

DPDP requirement: Each consent item must be individually revocable from Profile > Privacy at any time. Revoking face recognition consent = Rekognition data deleted within 24 hours (document this SLA in the privacy policy).

### Journey 4: Principal Morning Check-In (Admin Dashboard)

```
Principal opens dashboard (web browser, mobile or desktop)
  → Engagement score hero: "73% ⬆️ (was 61% last week)"
  → 3 metric tiles: Posts today / Attendance today / Fee alerts
  → Class breakdown table (sorted by engagement %, lowest first)
  → One-click: message teacher of lowest-engagement class via WhatsApp
```

The "message teacher" shortcut is the key action. Don't make principals hunt for a phone number.

## Responsive & Accessibility Specifications

### React Native (Teacher + Parent apps)
- Minimum touch target: 44×44pt (Apple HIG) / 48×48dp (Material). Apply to all tappable elements.
- Keyboard accessibility: Form inputs must advance with `returnKeyType="next"`. Final input `returnKeyType="done"`.
- Dynamic Type: Use `allowFontScaling={true}` on all Text components. Test at 200% system font size.
- Screen reader: All interactive elements have `accessibilityLabel`. Photos have `accessibilityLabel` set to caption text.
- Color contrast: Text on background ≥ 4.5:1 (WCAG AA). Checked for all palette combinations above.
- Offline feedback: When network is unavailable, show persistent banner at top: "You're offline — showing saved content."

### Next.js Admin Dashboard (Web)
- Breakpoints: Mobile 375px, Tablet 768px, Desktop 1280px. Admin dashboard is usable at 375px (principals check on phones).
- Viewport hierarchy:
  - 1280px+: Sidebar nav + main content (two-column)
  - 768px–1279px: Collapsible sidebar or top nav
  - <768px: Bottom tab nav, stacked single column
- Keyboard navigation: All tables are keyboard-navigable. Focus ring visible (not removed with `outline: none`).
- Print view: Fee invoices and attendance reports must print cleanly (CSS `@media print`).

## AI Caption UX Guidelines

The AI caption is the product's most visible AI feature. These rules prevent it from feeling like a robot wrote it:

1. **Voice**: Warm, observational, parent-first. "Rohan was counting along today!" NOT "Student demonstrated numerical sequencing behavior."
2. **Length**: 1–2 sentences maximum. Parents read on a phone, not a laptop.
3. **Never hallucinate children**: Caption draft may only name children that Rekognition identified with > 80% confidence AND teacher confirmed via tag. Unconfirmed children: "the children" or omit names.
4. **Teacher edits**: The draft is always editable. Edit history is logged (for DPDP audit trail — teacher last-edited counts as human review).
5. **Failed AI**: If Claude API times out or returns an error, show an empty caption box with placeholder: "What happened in this moment?" Never show an error message — just let the teacher type.
6. **Language**: English only at launch. When Hindi/Tamil added (Phase 2), caption language defaults to teacher's app language setting.
